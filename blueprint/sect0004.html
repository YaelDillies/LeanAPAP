5 Finite field model

Lemma 5.1
#

Let \(\epsilon {\gt}0\). If \(A,C\subseteq G\), where \(C\) has density at least \(\gamma \), then either

  1. \(\left\lvert \langle \mu _A\ast \mu _A,\mu _C\rangle -1\right\rvert \leq \epsilon \) or

  2. \(\lVert \mu _A\circ \mu _A-1\rVert _p \geq \epsilon /2\) for some \(p\ll \log (2.\gamma )\).

Proof ▼

If the first alternative fails, then by Hölder’s inequality, for any \(p\geq 1\)

\[ \epsilon {\lt} \left\lvert \langle \mu _A \ast \mu _A - 1, \mu _C \rangle \right\rvert \leq \lVert \mu _A\ast \mu _A-1\rVert _p\gamma ^{-1/p}. \]

In particular if we choose \(p=2\lceil K\log {\gamma }\rceil \) for some large constant \(K\) then we deduce that

\[ \lVert \mu _A\ast \mu _A-1\rVert _{p}\geq \tfrac {1}{2}\epsilon . \]

It remains to note that, since \(p\) is even,

\[ \lVert \mu _A\ast \mu _A-1\rVert _{p}^p=(\widehat{\mu _A}^21_{\neq 0})^{(p)}(0)\leq (\lvert \widehat{\mu _A} \rvert ^21_{\neq 0})^{(p)}(0)=\lVert \mu _A\circ \mu _A-1\rVert _p^p. \qedhere \]

Proposition 5.2
#

Let \(\epsilon \in (0,1)\). If \(A,C\subseteq \mathbb {F}_q^n\), where \(C\) has density at least \(\gamma \), then either

  1. \(\left\lvert \langle \mu _A\ast \mu _A,\mu _C\rangle -1\right\rvert \leq \epsilon \) or

  2. there is a subspace \(V\) of codimension

    \[ \ll _\epsilon \log (2/\gamma )^4\log (2/\alpha )^4 \]

    such that \(\lVert 1_{A}\ast \mu _V\rVert _\infty \geq (1+\Omega (\epsilon ))\alpha \).

Proof ▼

By Lemma ??, if the first alternative fails then \(\lVert \mu _A\circ \mu _A-1\rVert _p\geq \epsilon /2\) for some \(p\ll \mathcal{L}{\gamma }\). By Lemma ?? (applied to \(f=\mu _A\circ \mu _A-1\)) we deduce that \(\lVert \mu _A\circ \mu _A\rVert _p\geq 1+\epsilon /4\) for some \(p\ll _\epsilon \mathcal{L}{\gamma }\). Hence, by Corollary ??, there are \(A_1,A_2\), both of density

\[ \geq \alpha ^{O_\epsilon (\mathcal{L}{\gamma })}, \]

such that \(\langle \mu _{A_1}\circ \mu _{A_2},1_{S}\rangle \geq 1-\epsilon /32\), where \(S=\{ x : \mu _A\circ \mu _A(x)\geq 1+\epsilon /8\} \) . By Theorem ?? there is a subspace \(V\) of the required codimension such that

\[ \langle \mu _V\ast \mu _{A_1}\circ \mu _{A_2},1_{S}\rangle \geq 1-\tfrac {1}{16}\epsilon . \]

By definition of \(S\), it follows that

\begin{align*} 1+\Omega (\epsilon ) & \leq (1+\epsilon /8)(1-\epsilon /16)\\ & \leq \langle \mu _V\ast \mu _{A_1}\circ \mu _{A_2},\mu _A\circ \mu _A\rangle \\ & \leq \lVert \mu _V\ast \mu _A\rVert _\infty \lVert \mu _{A}\ast \mu _{A_2}\circ \mu _{A_1}\rVert _1\\ & = \lVert \mu _V\ast 1_{A}\rVert _\infty \alpha ^{-1}, \end{align*}

and the proof is complete.
